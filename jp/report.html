日本住宅房地產市場報告

2026年3月 · 市場研究

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY執行摘要

Japan's residential property market in 2025 presents one of the most compelling investment narratives in Asia Pacific: a structurally undersupplied urban market, the world's most accommodative monetary policy turning gradually tighter, and a weaker yen attracting record foreign capital. The national condominium price index rose 8.28% year-on-year, while Tokyo's new-build prices surged 20%+ in peak months. Fukuoka has emerged as the standout performer with 21.8% YoY growth and rental yields of 4.98% — the best combination of income and appreciation in Japan.

The central risk is BOJ rate normalization: at 0.5%, financing costs remain extraordinarily low, but any acceleration toward 1.5–2.0% would materially compress the positive carry that has driven urban price inflation for the past decade. The national rental yield of 4.34% produces a spread of +3.84% over the policy rate — far more attractive than Taiwan's +0.24% or even Malaysia's +2.44%. For foreign investors, Japan offers the rare combination of institutional-grade transparency, yield support, and currency discount.

日本住宅市場在2025年呈現亞太地區最引人注目的投資敘事之一：結構性供不應求的都市市場、全球最寬鬆的貨幣政策正逐步收緊、以及弱勢日圓吸引創紀錄的外資。全國公寓價格指數年增8.28%，東京新建案在高峰月份漲幅超過20%。福岡以21.8%的年成長率和4.98%的租金收益率脫穎而出——是日本收租與增值兼具的最佳組合。

核心風險在於日銀升息正常化：政策利率0.5%下，融資成本仍極度低廉，但若加速升至1.5到2.0%，將大幅壓縮過去十年驅動都市房價上漲的正利差。全國租金收益率4.34%與政策利率之間的利差達+3.84%——遠優於台灣的+0.24%甚至馬來西亞的+2.44%。對外國投資人而言，日本提供了機構級透明度、收益率支撐與匯率折價的稀有組合。

BOJ RATE NORMALIZATIONBOJ升息正常化

The Bank of Japan has moved from -0.1% to 0.5% in three hikes, ending the world's last negative-rate regime. The path has been deliberate: exiting negative rates in March 2024, lifting to 0.25% in July 2024, and reaching 0.5% in January 2025. This normalization fundamentally alters the financing calculus for Japanese real estate, though the impact remains modest in absolute terms.

A 35-year mortgage now costs approximately 1.5–2.0%, still extraordinarily cheap by global standards. Against a national rental yield of 4.34%, this produces a positive carry spread of +2.84% at the upper bound of mortgage rates — comfortably above the breakeven threshold. The key risk is pace rather than direction: gradual normalization toward 1.0–1.5% is manageable, but any acceleration beyond market expectations would compress spreads and trigger repricing in rate-sensitive segments.

日本銀行已透過三次升息，從-0.1%升至0.5%，終結了全球最後一個負利率政策。升息路徑循序漸進：2024年3月退出負利率，2024年7月升至0.25%，2025年1月達到0.5%。這一正常化根本性地改變了日本房地產的融資計算，儘管絕對水準的衝擊仍然有限。

35年期房貸利率目前約1.5到2.0%，以全球標準仍屬極低。對照全國4.34%的租金收益率，在房貸利率上限下仍產生+2.84%的正利差——遠高於損益平衡閾值。關鍵風險在於速度而非方向：逐步升至1.0到1.5%可控，但若超出市場預期的加速升息，將壓縮利差並觸發利率敏感板塊的重新定價。

SUPPLY: SHRINKING STARTS AND THE AKIYA PARADOX供給：開工萎縮與空屋悖論

Japan's housing starts have been in secular decline, falling from 905,000 units in 2019 to an estimated 760,000 in 2025 — a drop of -4.6% year-on-year. The decline is driven by demographics and rising construction costs, which have surged 25–29% since 2021 due to materials inflation and a chronic labor shortage in the building trades. Developers are prioritizing margin over volume across all three major metros.

The akiya (vacant home) crisis presents a paradox: nationally, 13.8% of homes are vacant — over 9 million units — yet these are overwhelmingly located in rural and depopulating areas with zero investment relevance. The urban-rural mismatch is extreme: in the three major metro areas where investment demand concentrates, vacancy is structurally low and new supply is constrained. Construction cost escalation acts as a floor under new-build pricing, as developers cannot profitably deliver units below replacement cost.

日本新建開工數持續下降，從2019年的90.5萬戶降至2025年預估的76萬戶，年減-4.6%。下降的驅動因素是人口結構與建設成本飆升——自2021年以來因建材通膨和建築業長期缺工已上漲25到29%。三大都會區的開發商均優先追求利潤而非銷量。

空屋問題呈現一個悖論：全國空屋率高達13.8%，超過900萬戶，但這些空屋絕大多數位於人口外流的鄉村地區，對投資毫無意義。城鄉落差極為懸殊：在投資需求集中的三大都會區，空置率在結構上偏低，新供給也受限。建設成本的攀升為新建案價格提供了底部支撐，因為開發商無法在低於重置成本的情況下獲利交付。

DEMAND: FOREIGN CAPITAL AND RESALE BOOM需求：外資湧入與中古屋榮景

Foreign capital has become the single most important marginal demand driver in Japan's urban property markets. The weak yen has made Japanese real estate extraordinarily cheap in foreign currency terms. Foreign buyer share in Tokyo's luxury segment has reached an estimated 27%, up from just 12% in 2020. Taiwanese, Mainland Chinese, Hong Kong, and Singaporean buyers dominate the cross-border flow, drawn by yield spreads unavailable in their home markets.

The domestic resale market is booming in parallel. Pre-owned condominium prices in Greater Tokyo surged +27.17% in H1 2025, reflecting genuine end-user demand from dual-income households upgrading within the metro area. The weak yen effect is a double catalyst: it attracts foreign capital while simultaneously making imports more expensive, pushing domestic buyers toward existing housing stock rather than new-builds with imported materials.

外資已成為日本都市房市最重要的邊際需求驅動力。弱勢日圓使日本房地產以外幣計價極為便宜。東京豪宅市場的外資買家佔比已從2020年的12%攀升至27%。台灣、中國大陸、香港和新加坡買家主導跨境資金流入，被自己市場無法獲得的利差所吸引。

國內中古屋市場同步蓬勃發展。首都圈中古公寓價格在2025年上半年飆升+27.17%，反映的是雙薪家庭在都會區換屋的真實自住需求。弱勢日圓形成雙重催化：既吸引外資，又因進口成本上升推動國內買家轉向現有房源，而非使用進口建材的新建案。

VALUATION估值

Japan's national rental yield of 4.34% against a BOJ policy rate of 0.5% produces a yield spread of +3.84% — the widest positive carry in developed Asia. This spread is the fundamental valuation anchor: as long as it remains above +2.0%, the income case for Japanese property holds regardless of capital appreciation trends. By comparison, Taiwan's spread is a razor-thin +0.24% and Malaysia's is +2.44%.

In an APAC price-to-rent comparison, Tokyo sits at 37x — elevated but justified by the near-zero financing cost environment. Compared to Taipei at 47x and Hong Kong at 50x, Tokyo offers materially better value. The carry advantage makes Japan uniquely attractive for leveraged income strategies, with Fukuoka and Osaka offering the best value entry points in the Japanese market.

日本全國租金收益率4.34%，扣除BOJ政策利率0.5%後，利差達+3.84%——是已開發亞洲最大的正利差。這個利差是根本的估值錨定：只要維持在+2.0%以上，日本房產的收租邏輯就成立，無論資本增值趨勢如何。相比之下，台灣利差僅+0.24%，馬來西亞為+2.44%。

在亞太區域房價租金比的比較中，東京37倍雖偏高，但在近零融資成本環境下仍屬合理。相較於台北47倍和香港50倍，東京的估值明顯更具價值。利差優勢使日本對槓桿收租策略格外具有吸引力，其中福岡和大阪是日本市場中最佳的價值進入點。

TOKYO 23 WARDS東京23區

Tokyo's 23-ward area continues to set records. The average new-build condominium price reached ¥94 million in 2025, with a peak month breaching the psychological ¥100 million threshold. Land prices rose +7.9% year-on-year, the highest appreciation among Japan's major markets, reflecting persistent demand for central urban sites.

The rental market is equally buoyant. Average asking rents hit ¥4,332/sqm, up +6.4% year-on-year, driven by tight vacancy and strong demand from both domestic professionals and the growing expatriate population. The combination of rising rents and appreciating land values underpins the total return thesis for Tokyo's core wards, even as entry prices reach historically elevated levels.

東京23區持續刷新紀錄。2025年新建公寓均價達9,400萬日圓，高峰月份突破1億日圓的心理關口。地價年增+7.9%，為日本主要市場中最高漲幅，反映了核心都市用地的持續需求。

租賃市場同樣強勁。平均招租租金達每平方公尺4,332日圓，年增+6.4%，受惠於低空置率以及國內專業人士和日益增長的外派人口的強勁需求。租金上漲與地價增值的組合支撐了東京核心區的總回報邏輯，即便入場價格已達歷史高位。

FUKUOKA福岡

Fukuoka has delivered 13 consecutive years of price appreciation, the longest winning streak in Japan. The average condominium price reached ¥68 million in 2025, up +21.8% year-on-year, outpacing both Tokyo and Osaka. The city's population continues to grow — one of the few major Japanese cities with positive net migration — driven by a thriving startup ecosystem, Kyushu's role as a semiconductor hub, and quality of life.

The investment case for Fukuoka is the yield story. At 4.98%, Fukuoka's rental yield is the highest among Japan's major cities. The Tenjin Big Bang urban renewal project is transforming the city center with Grade-A office, retail, and mixed-use developments, further reinforcing Fukuoka's credentials as Japan's most dynamic secondary market. Combined with the strongest capital appreciation, Fukuoka offers total returns that no other Japanese city can match.

福岡已連續13年房價上漲，為日本最長連漲紀錄。2025年公寓均價達6,800萬日圓，年增+21.8%，超越東京和大阪。福岡人口持續成長——是日本少數維持淨遷入的主要城市之一——受益於蓬勃的新創生態圈、九州作為半導體製造中心的角色，以及優質的生活品質。

福岡的投資邏輯是收益率。4.98%的租金收益率是日本主要城市中最高。天神Big Bang都市更新計畫正以甲級辦公室、零售和複合用途開發改造市中心，進一步鞏固福岡作為日本最具活力的二線城市的地位。加上最強的資本增值，福岡提供的總回報是其他日本城市無法匹敵的。

OUTLOOK展望

Bull case: BOJ holds at 0.5% through 2026, yen stays weak (150+/USD), foreign inflows accelerate. National prices +8–12%, Fukuoka +15%+. Tokyo new-builds breach ¥100M sustained average.

Base case: BOJ hikes to 0.75% by year-end, yen drifts to 140–145. National prices +5–8%, continued resale strength. Yield spread narrows but remains above +3.0% — still attractive for income strategies.

Bear case: BOJ accelerates to 1.0%+, yen recovers to 125–130, foreign demand retreats. National prices flat to +2%, Tokyo luxury corrects 5–10%. Even here, rental income provides a floor absent in speculative markets.

The primary wildcard is yen direction. A rapid yen recovery would simultaneously reduce foreign buying power and signal tighter BOJ policy — a double headwind. Conversely, continued yen weakness extends the most favorable conditions for cross-border investment in a generation.

樂觀情境：BOJ在2026年全年維持0.5%，日圓持續走弱（150+兌1美元），外資加速流入。全國房價+8到12%，福岡+15%以上。東京新建案均價持續突破1億日圓。

基本情境：BOJ年底前升至0.75%，日圓緩步回升至140到145。全國房價+5到8%，中古屋市場持續強勁。利差收窄但仍維持+3.0%以上——對收租策略仍具吸引力。

悲觀情境：BOJ加速升至1.0%以上，日圓回升至125到130，外資需求退潮。全國房價持平至+2%，東京豪宅修正5到10%。即便如此，租金收入仍提供了投機市場所缺乏的底部支撐。

最大的不確定因素是日圓走向。日圓若快速回升，將同時削弱外資購買力並暗示BOJ進一步緊縮——形成雙重逆風。反之，日圓持續走弱則延續了一個世代以來最有利的跨境投資條件。
