EXECUTIVE SUMMARY執行摘要

Malaysia's residential property market entered 2026 carrying the most complex supply overhang since the 2016–2019 peak cycle. The headline numbers tell a bifurcated story: gross rental yields of 5.19% on residential property continue to produce a positive carry spread of +2.19% above the OPR of 3.0%, making Malaysia one of the few Asian markets where property still generates positive real income. Yet the same quarter — Q3 2025 — saw national residential Overhang surge 30.5% year-on-year to 28,672 units, reversing three consecutive years of improvement. When Malaysia's commercial-classified serviced apartment Overhang of 17,892 units is added, the wide-definition national completed-unsold inventory reaches 46,564 units — a level that has no modern precedent.

The most important analytical correction is the serviced apartment reclassification of 2016. When NAPIC moved serviced apartments from the residential to the commercial property category, the official residential Overhang statistic became systematically understated. A market observer looking only at the residential Overhang figure of 28,672 would conclude that Johor's position — at 3,293 residential units — is problematic but manageable. Adding the SA Overhang of 9,018 units (50.4% of the national total) reveals that Johor's combined completed-unsold exposure of 12,688 units is more than 56% larger than Kuala Lumpur's combined figure of 8,101 units, which is the second most stressed market. This single misreading — treating residential Overhang as the whole picture — is the most common analytical error in Malaysian property research.

The structural dichotomy is product-based, not geography-based. Across all three major markets covered in this report — Kuala Lumpur, Penang, and Johor Bahru — the demand failure is almost exclusively a high-rise condominium and serviced apartment problem. Landed residential (terraced houses, semi-detached) consistently shows Overhang rates below 10% in all three cities, supported by genuine owner-occupier demand that is structurally indifferent to the investment cycle. The investment framework is therefore straightforward: avoid high-rise in oversupplied segments, favour landed in under-supplied zones, and monitor the three catalysts — RTS Link commissioning, JS-SEZ enterprise deployment, and KL serviced apartment NC pipeline approval decisions — that will determine whether the current supply stress self-corrects by 2028 or persists into the next decade.

馬來西亞住宅房地產市場以2016至2019年高峰週期以來最複雜的供給積壓態勢進入2026年。表面數字呈現兩極格局：住宅毛租金收益率5.19%持續提供高於OPR（3.0%）+2.19%的正利差，使馬來西亞成為亞洲少數房地產仍能產生正實際收入的市場之一。然而同一季度——2025年第三季——全國住宅Overhang年增30.5%至28,672戶，逆轉了連續三年的改善趨勢。加入商業分類的服務式公寓Overhang17,892戶後，廣義全國已完工未售庫存達46,564戶——一個現代史上前所未見的水平。

最重要的分析修正是2016年的服務式公寓重新分類。NAPIC將服務式公寓從住宅類移至商業物業類後，官方住宅Overhang統計數據開始系統性低估。只看住宅Overhang數字28,672戶的市場觀察者，會得出柔佛的3,293戶住宅問題「嚴重但可控」的結論。加入SA Overhang9,018戶（佔全國50.4%）後，柔佛合計已完工未售敞口12,688戶，比排名第二的吉隆坡合計8,101戶大出56%以上。這單一誤讀——將住宅Overhang視為全貌——是馬來西亞房地產研究中最常見的分析錯誤。

結構性分化是按產品類型劃分，而非按地域劃分。在本報告涵蓋的三個主要市場——吉隆坡、檳城、新山——需求失敗幾乎全部是高層Condo和服務式公寓問題。有地住宅（排屋、半獨立式）在三個城市的Overhang率一致低於10%，受結構性不依賴投資週期的真實自住需求支撐。投資框架因此清晰：迴避供給過剩細分市場的高層住宅，偏好供應不足區域的有地住宅，並監測三個催化劑——RTS Link通車、JS-SEZ企業落地、以及KL服務式公寓NC管道審批決定——這三者將決定當前供給壓力是在2028年前自我修正，還是持續進入下一個十年。

SUPPLY: THE DELIVERY WAVE供給面：完工交屋潮尚未退去

The supply challenge facing Malaysia's residential market is best understood through the lens of a time-lagged system with locked-in consequences. A residential project commenced in 2024 cannot be un-built. The 106,236 units started that year — a 20.6% increase over 2023 — will arrive in the market between 2026 and 2028 regardless of what happens to demand in the interim. This is the completion wave, and it is the single most important supply-side variable for the next two years.

The 2025 data shows the absorption system already under stress. In Q1–Q3 2025, 69,303 units were completed and received their Certificate of Completion and Compliance, a 25.3% year-on-year increase. High-rise developments now take 18–24 months to achieve meaningful sales penetration — three to four times the six-month benchmark. The unsold completed stock reached 28,672 residential units worth RM17.25 billion as of Q3 2025, rising to 44,794 units when serviced apartments are included, up 30.5% year-on-year.

The developer response has been rational and relatively swift. New residential launches in the first nine months of 2025 fell 31.8% year-on-year to 24,913 units. New planned supply contracted 31.7%. Only 41% of developers planned new launches in H2 2025. This pullback is the necessary precondition for eventual supply tightening — but the units already under construction cannot be recalled. The completion wave is locked in through 2027.

The serviced apartment segment deserves specific attention. Developers historically favored this format because commercial land classification exempts them from affordable housing quota requirements. The result was a sustained wave of supply in the RM500,000–RM1,000,000 band across KL, JB, and Penang that bears limited relationship to actual demand. The 17,892 unsold serviced apartment units as of Q3 2025 carry commercial utility rates typically four to six times higher than residential tariffs, complex financing conditions, and maintenance cost structures that erode the yield advantage used to market these units. Clearing this inventory will require either extended time or significant structural repricing.

The outlook for overhang depends critically on absorption. Under the base case (absorption held at 85,000 units/year, consistent with the five-year average), overhang peaks at approximately 47,000 units in 2027 before declining to around 14,000 by 2029 — a level consistent with healthy market inventory of roughly two months' supply. The bull case (absorption rising to 90,000/year on sustained OPR stability and GDP above 5%) sees the peak contained at 42,000 units and a faster recovery to approximately 8,000 units by 2029. The bear case (absorption falling to 75,000/year under OPR reversal or economic deceleration below 4% GDP) produces a peak of 57,000 units in 2027 that remains stubbornly elevated at 28,000 through 2029. All three scenarios share the same completion curve, derived from lagged 2024–2025 starts data — what differs is only the demand side.

2024年動工了106,236戶，這些房子你跑不掉，2026到2028年都會推出來。光是2025年前三季就完工交屋了69,303戶，比去年多出25.3%。高樓住宅從推案到去化，現在平均要18到24個月，是正常標準的三到四倍。截至2025年Q3，含服務式公寓的總待售餘屋已經堆到44,794套，年增30.5%。

服務式公寓是最大的地雷。建商愛蓋是因為可以繞開可負擔房屋配額，結果在吉隆坡、新山、檳城50萬到100萬令吉價格帶，堆出一大堆沒人要的庫存。現在積壓的17,892套服務式公寓，不只定價過高，商業水電費率還比住宅貴四到六倍，貸款條件更嚴苛。這批庫存要清，不是降價就是等時間，短期都不是利多。

但建商已經在踩煞車了——2025年前三季新推案量年減31.8%，規劃量也少了31.7%，只有41%的業者說下半年還會推案。這代表2028年之後新供給會明顯減少，市場有機會重新平衡。

這代表什麼？未來兩年交屋潮躲不掉，但最壞的時候快過了。基本情境下餘屋在2027年觸頂約47,000套，之後開始下滑。如果你打算2027年之後進場，到時候的供給壓力會比現在輕很多。但如果你現在就想買，就要挑對產品——避開服務式公寓和外圍高樓，選成熟走廊的有地住宅或中價位公寓。

NATIONAL SUPPLY PIPELINE: THE SERVICED APARTMENT BLIND SPOT 全國在建供給：服務式公寓的統計盲點

Malaysia's total unsold property supply pipeline as of Q3 2025 reaches 209,380 units across all categories — residential, serviced apartments, SOHO, and commercial shophouses. The residential segment accounts for 53.7% (112,411 units across Overhang, under-construction, and not-yet-started). The serviced apartment pipeline at 74,836 units represents 35.7% of the total — a structurally significant share for a product type that has been reclassified as "commercial" since 2016 and therefore disappears from residential Overhang statistics. The critical embedded risk is the serviced apartment under-construction pipeline: 44,035 SA units are currently under construction nationally, representing 63.5% of the equivalent residential figure. Selangor alone accounts for 18,116 units of SA under-construction, while KL's SA pipeline (8,212 under-construction + 9,415 planned = 17,627 units) represents the most concentrated future supply pressure in a single urban area anywhere in Malaysia.

The serviced apartment Overhang has a structural clearing problem that residential Overhang does not share. Residential units benefit from first-home buyer schemes, stamp duty exemptions, 90% LTV mortgages for owner-occupiers, and Bumiputera quota absorption. Serviced apartments — commercially titled — are ineligible for all of these. The average SA Overhang clearing cycle documented in Malaysian academic research is 3–5 years versus 1–2 years for residential. Nationally, 60% of SA Overhang is priced above RM500,000, a level that exceeds the organic purchasing power of the local salaried market and makes the SA clearing mechanism almost entirely dependent on foreign investors and expatriate tenants.

截至2025年第三季，馬來西亞各類別未售物業在建供給合計209,380戶——住宅、服務式公寓、SOHO及商業店舖。住宅佔53.7%（Overhang、在建、未建合計112,411戶）。服務式公寓管道74,836戶佔35.7%——對一個自2016年起被重分類為「商業」、因此從住宅Overhang統計中消失的產品類型而言，這是結構性顯著的比例。核心隱藏風險是服務式公寓在建供給：全國目前有44,035戶SA在建，相當於住宅在建量的63.5%。單是雪蘭莪就有18,116戶SA在建，而KL的SA管道（在建8,212戶+規劃9,415戶=17,627戶）代表馬來西亞任何單一城市最集中的未來供給壓力。

服務式公寓Overhang面臨住宅Overhang不具備的結構性出清問題。住宅單位受益於首購族計劃、印花稅豁免、自住90% LTV房貸，以及土著配額吸收。商業地契的服務式公寓——完全不符合上述所有資格。馬來西亞學術研究記錄的SA Overhang平均清貨週期為3至5年，對比住宅的1至2年。全國60%的SA Overhang定價超過RM50萬，這一水平超過了本地薪資市場的有機購買力，使SA出清機制幾乎完全依賴外國投資者和外籍租客。

DEMAND: INTENT WITHOUT ACCESS需求面：想買的人很多，能成交的不夠

The demand picture for Malaysian residential property is more nuanced than either the headline transaction figures or the overhang statistics suggest. The 420,525 transactions recorded in 2024 represent the highest annual volume in a decade, demonstrating that underlying purchase intent is real and persistent. The cooling in Q3 2025 to 108,250 transactions does not represent demand destruction — it represents the progressive exhaustion of the most financially capable segment of latent demand, as the remaining buyers increasingly fail the financing filter.

The most revealing indicator is not transaction volume but take-up rate at new launches. In Q1 2025, only 10.8% of new residential units launched were sold on launch day. For high-rise projects in Q3 2025, that figure was 5.1%. Landed properties performed better at 14%. The divergence maps almost exactly onto loan approval rates: landed buyers have stronger financial profiles and clearer income documentation. A take-up rate below 10% at launch is not a sign of disinterest — it is a sign that the majority of potential participants have been structurally filtered out by the financing system.

The loan approval rate of 42.6% means that more than half of all mortgage applications are declined. Malaysia's household debt-to-GDP ratio of 81.9% is among the highest in ASEAN, and debt-service ratios in Klang Valley and Penang are at their practical limits for M40 households. The government's SJKP guarantee scheme — with RM10 billion in guarantees targeting approximately 20,000 first-time buyers — provides incremental improvement but addresses financing access at the margin rather than the underlying affordability gap.

The K-shaped structure of demand defines the opportunity set. In the sub-RM300,000 segment, genuine undersupply exists in locations with real employment anchors. The RM300,000–RM500,000 band shows the healthiest primary market take-up. Above RM500,000 in high-rise and serviced apartment formats, demand is institutionally constrained by the financing filter. The secondary market's 79.4% share of H1 2025 residential transactions is the direct expression of buyers gravitating toward segments where value, location quality, and financing accessibility intersect most favorably.

Kuala Lumpur's transaction market shows resilience at the top end — 18,913 units changed hands in 2024, a decade high, with the above-RM1 million segment recording the fastest growth at +31% year-on-year to 3,079 transactions. This bifurcation — record luxury volumes alongside a stuck loan approval rate of 42.6% — captures the structural inequality at the heart of the KL market: those with equity can transact; those relying on credit cannot.

2024年吉隆坡賣了18,913套，創十年新高，但不要高興太早——貸款核准率只有42.6%，超過一半的人被銀行拒絕。420,525套的全國成交量看起來很漂亮，但2025年Q3已經降到108,250筆，不是大家不想買，是財務條件好的那批人已經買完了。

新案子首日去化率最能說明問題。2025年Q3，高樓住宅首日只賣出5.1%，有地住宅好一點是14%。去化率這麼低不是沒人要，是大多數想買的人被貸款門檻擋住了。全國家庭負債佔GDP高達81.9%，很多家庭現有月供已經到極限，再借一筆房貸銀行根本不會批。政府的信用保證計畫（SJKP）方向對了，但100億令吉只能幫到約兩萬人，杯水車薪。

更值得注意的是市場的K型分化。逾100萬令吉的豪宅成交年增31%至3,079宗，破紀錄。但同時貸款核准率卡在42.6%——有資產的人在買，靠貸款的人買不了。中古屋佔成交量79.4%，是因為買家知道在地段成熟的二手市場，才最容易找到划算又貸得到款的選擇。

真正的需求在30萬令吉以下（佔所有買方的53.1%），偏偏供給最少在那裡。30到50萬令吉的中間帶需求最健康，50萬以上的高樓被貸款門檻卡死。這對你的影響：不要追高價位新推案，除非你是現金買家或財務條件非常好。一般投資人最好的機會在30到80萬令吉的中古屋市場。

VALUATION: TWO FRAMEWORKS, TWO CONCLUSIONS估值：兩個框架，兩個結論

Malaysian residential property presents a bifurcated valuation picture that produces diametrically opposed conclusions depending on the analytical framework applied — and both conclusions are simultaneously correct for different buyer populations.

From a yield and regional comparison perspective, the investment case is reasonably compelling. The gross rental yield of 5.19% against an OPR of 2.75% produces a positive carry spread of +2.44% — historically a signal of relative value. Against regional peers, Kuala Lumpur's price-to-rent ratio of 21.7x is the lowest in this eight-city comparison — below Bangkok (25x), Tokyo (37x), Singapore (40x), Taipei (47x), Hong Kong (50x), and Shanghai (59x). Even Ho Chi Minh City at 18x is the only market that edges KL out on this metric. For yield-seeking capital rotating out of overpriced Asian markets, the relative value case is unambiguous.

From a domestic affordability perspective, however, the picture is materially less favorable. At 4.3x median annual household income nationally — rising to 5.6x for terrace houses in Kuala Lumpur — Malaysian housing is classified as "severely unaffordable" by the Demographia methodology. This ceiling is structural and will not be meaningfully moved by OPR adjustments alone.

The real house price picture adds important context. The MHPI has grown modestly in nominal terms — approximately 1–3% annually — but adjusted for CPI inflation, real house prices have been effectively flat to slightly declining since 2022. This is not a bubble profile; it is a market adjusting real valuations downward through inflation erosion while maintaining nominal stability.

The price-to-income lens tells a more sobering story. At 5.6x the median annual household income, Kuala Lumpur housing remains technically unaffordable by international benchmarks — though it still compares favorably against Taipei (12.1x), Tokyo (13.5x), and Singapore (14.2x). The more telling signal is the divergence between nominal and real house prices: the nominal MHPI has risen 42% since 2015, but the real inflation-adjusted index has moved only 24% over the same period. In the last five years, real prices have been essentially flat — meaning the affordability pain has been driven entirely by income stagnation and financing costs, not by house price appreciation.

外資看吉隆坡覺得便宜——房價租金比（P/R）21.7倍，新加坡是40倍、香港50倍、上海59倍。租金報酬率5.19%比銀行利率高出2.44個百分點，買房收租有正現金流。從亞洲區域比較的角度，吉隆坡和胡志明市（18倍）是僅有的兩個還算划算的城市。

但本地人的感受完全不同。吉隆坡房價是家庭年收入中位數的5.6倍，國際標準超過4倍就算「嚴重不可負擔」。這不只是統計數字——它直接解釋了為什麼貸款核准率只有42.6%。房價所得比跟台北（12.1倍）、東京（13.5倍）、新加坡（14.2倍）比起來算溫和，但對本地人來說根本買不起就是買不起。

過去五年實質房價幾乎沒漲——名義房價指數（MHPI）自2015年來漲了42%，但扣掉通膨後實質只漲了24%。也就是說，可負擔性惡化不是房價飆漲造成的，而是收入成長停滯和融資成本上升的結果。這不是泡沫，是一個在通膨侵蝕下悄悄調整實質價格的市場。

這對收租型投資人的意義：房價沒在漲但租金一直在漲，房價租金比正在壓縮到近年最有吸引力的水準。如果你的策略是買來收租而不是等增值，現在的估值環境是五年來最好的。但如果你是本地首購族，除非收入在前20%，否則在現行貸款條件下進場會非常辛苦。

KUALA LUMPUR: CORRECTION AND OPPORTUNITY吉隆坡：修正即機會

Kuala Lumpur's residential market in 2025–2026 is defined by a structural bifurcation that aggregate data consistently obscures. The headline Overhang figure of 2,287 units as of Q3 2025 — down 37.2% from H1 2025's 3,643 units — appears to signal recovery. It does not. The official Overhang number counts only freehold residential-titled completed units unsold for over nine months. It excludes the estimated 8,000–9,000 units of serviced apartment inventory under commercial title — a product category that accounts for the majority of KL's actual unsold high-rise stock. The true wide-definition Overhang across KL is estimated at 10,300–11,300 units, a figure that tells a very different story.

At the same time, KL's demand fundamentals are structurally improving at the high end. Total residential transactions reached 18,913 units in 2024 — up 5.5% year-on-year — but the value of those transactions rose 16.6% to RM17.46 billion, indicating buyers are systematically upgrading to higher price points. The RM1 million-plus segment grew 31% in volume to 3,079 units, driven by an emerging institutional demand channel: the MM2H programme, which has 2,637 pre-approved buyers representing an estimated RM2.3 billion in latent purchasing power yet to be deployed. KL's price advantage versus Singapore — where equivalent quality high-rise stock trades at a 87% premium — continues to attract USD and SGD capital seeking Asian real estate exposure at a relative discount.

The critical analytical framework is not "supply vs demand in aggregate" but rather three distinct market layers operating simultaneously. The sub-RM500K segment suffers from structural undersupply of genuine residential-titled product as developers exit uneconomic price points. The RM500K–RM1M segment is the most stressed, dominated by legacy commercial-title serviced apartments with structural buyer resistance from commercial utility rates and absence of HDA protection. The RM1M-plus segment shows the healthiest supply-demand dynamics, with limited premium residential-titled pipeline in KLCC, Bangsar, and Mont Kiara facing genuine scarcity against growing foreign demand. Understanding these three layers is the prerequisite for any credible KL market assessment.

The composition of KL's official 2,287-unit Overhang as of Q3 2025 reveals a market with two structurally distinct problems. The first is legacy affordable stock: the RM300K–500K band accounts for 900 units (39.3%) of total Overhang, primarily commercial-title condominium projects in Cheras, Kepong, and Setapak built during the 2016–2019 affordable high-rise construction wave. These units suffer from the structural triple-penalty of commercial utility rates, no HDA buyer protection, and neighbourhood over-concentration. Take-up rates in this band sit at 60–75%, significantly below the market average of 86.2%. The second problem is high-end stagnation: the RM1M-plus band carries 478 units at an average unit value of approximately RM1.05 million, representing 63% of total Overhang value (RM15.1 billion of RM24 billion total). These are predominantly KLCC and Bangsar luxury units where buyer selectivity — not lack of demand — drives the extended marketing period. The clearing dynamic here is time, not price: developers with adequate financing will hold rather than discount.

BROAD INVENTORY: THE SERVICED APARTMENT OVERHANG 廣義庫存：服務式公寓的隱形壓力

The most important number missing from Malaysia's official property statistics is the serviced apartment Overhang. Under NAPIC's classification, serviced apartments — which are commercially titled high-rise units marketed as residential — are tracked separately under commercial property statistics. As of Q3 2025, national serviced apartment inventory stands at 17,900 units. Based on KL's historical share of approximately 45% of total national serviced apartment stock, KL's implied exposure is 8,000–9,000 units — more than three times the official residential Overhang figure. Adding these to the official 2,287-unit residential Overhang gives a wide-definition KL Overhang of approximately 10,300–11,300 units.

The serviced apartment overhang is structurally the hardest to clear. Three permanent disadvantages create persistent buyer resistance: commercial utility rates that run approximately six times higher than residential rates; the absence of Housing Development Act protection, leaving buyers with no statutory recourse in defect or abandonment scenarios; and the inability to access government-subsidised home financing schemes. For the RM500K–RM1M segment in particular — which represents 53% of the national serviced apartment inventory — these structural penalties are likely permanent unless a commercial-to-residential title conversion policy is implemented, which remains speculative. Investors holding legacy serviced apartment units should model cash flows assuming rental income as the only exit, not capital appreciation.

馬來西亞官方物業統計中最重要的缺失數字，是服務式公寓的Overhang。在NAPIC的分類體系下，服務式公寓——以商業地契登記、以住宅方式行銷的高層公寓——被獨立列入商業物業統計，而非住宅統計。截至2025年第三季，全國服務式公寓庫存達17,900戶。根據KL歷史上佔全國服務式公寓庫存約45%的比例，KL的估算敞口為8,000至9,000戶——超過官方住宅Overhang數字的三倍。將此加入官方2,287戶住宅Overhang，KL廣義Overhang估計達10,300至11,300戶。

服務式公寓庫存在結構上最難消化。三項永久性劣勢形成持久的買方抗拒：商業水電費率比住宅高約六倍；缺乏《住宅發展法》保護，買家在瑕疵或爛尾情況下無法律追索；以及無法使用政府補貼的住宅融資方案。對RM50至100萬段而言——此段佔全國服務式公寓庫存的53%——這些結構性懲罰可能是永久性的，除非商業地契轉住宅地契的轉換政策得以實施（目前仍屬推測）。持有歷史遺留服務式公寓單位的投資人，應以租金收入為唯一出口來建構現金流模型，而非押注資本增值。

Penang's residential market presents a more complex supply risk profile than its headline Overhang figure of 2,730 units (Q3 2025) suggests. The official number is manageable in isolation. The problem lies in the pipeline: under-construction unsold inventory stands at 7,804 units — larger than Kuala Lumpur's equivalent figure despite Penang's substantially smaller market. Annual condo transactions in Penang run at approximately 3,252 units on an annualised basis (Q3 2025: 813 units × 4). The under-construction pipeline alone represents 2.4 years of condo absorption capacity. The conversion risk is concentrated in a single price band: the RM400K–500K segment accounts for 3,169 units of under-construction inventory — 40.6% of the total — and is the segment most likely to convert to Overhang as completions accelerate through 2026–2027.

The structural duality of Penang's market is sharper than any other Malaysian state. Penang Island — specifically Timur Laut (George Town northeast) and Barat Daya (southwest) — carries 71.9% of all Overhang despite being a constrained land market with genuine scarcity fundamentals. The island's gross supply pipeline is physically limited by geography, and premium locations such as Gurney Drive, Tanjung Tokong, and the E&O Andaman Island development represent genuinely non-replicable addresses. Yet the majority of the Overhang and pending pipeline is concentrated in mid-market condominiums in these same areas — units priced at RM400K–700K that face direct competition from the resale market without the scarcity argument that protects premium stock. Seberang Perai (the mainland) carries only 28% of Overhang but is absorbing new FDI employment from Intel, NXP, and the emerging Silicon Island development — making it the market segment most likely to surprise to the upside in demand terms over the next three years.

Penang's investment thesis is ultimately a story of two markets forced to share the same address. The Overhang risk is real and concentrated in the RM400K–700K condo segment on the island; the appreciation thesis is intact and concentrated in the sub-RM500K landed segment in Seberang Perai and in premium island residential above RM1 million. The LRT — with civil works commenced in October 2025 and an estimated 2031 completion — represents the single most important medium-term demand catalyst for the Jelutong–Gelugor corridor. For investors entering ahead of the LRT timeline, the risk is execution delay; for those entering after, the concept premium will likely already be priced in.

JOHOR BAHRU新山

Johor Bahru occupies a structurally unique position in Southeast Asian real estate: it is the only city in the world with pedestrian and short-form land access to one of the most expensive residential markets on earth. Singapore's median private residential price exceeds SGD2,000 per square foot; Johor Bahru's high-rise residential market averages RM450–500 per square foot — approximately SGD150–165 — a gap of over 12 times. Singapore's median employee income exceeds RM20,000 per month equivalent; JB's median is approximately RM3,000–4,000. This wage and price arbitrage, combined with the impending RTS Link that will compress the cross-border commute from 60–90 minutes to 5–6 minutes, is the single structural force that distinguishes JB from every other Malaysian market and justifies the premium now being paid in the Zone 1 RTS corridor.

The demand case is supported by three additional structural pillars. The Johor-Singapore Special Economic Zone (JS-SEZ), formally launched in January 2025 across a 3,288 km² footprint, is Malaysia's most significant industrial policy in a generation — with committed investment already exceeding the government's initial targets. The Forest City Special Financial Zone (SFZ), offering a 15-year zero income tax incentive for financial sector participants, creates a separate demand anchor for high-value professional tenants independent of the RTS commuter thesis. And Johor's manufacturing corridor — already hosting Intel, Western Digital, and a growing semiconductor supply chain — provides stable employment-driven demand that does not depend on the Singapore arbitrage at all.

The risk register, however, is equally distinctive. Johor carries the largest residential Overhang of any Malaysian state at 3,293 units as of Q3 2025, with an Overhang rate of 21% — nearly double the national average. The under-construction pipeline adds a further 8,183 units of residential unsold inventory, plus 1,316 units of commercial serviced apartments, for a combined supply pipeline of 14,627 units. JB City Centre alone accounts for 51% of state Overhang (1,683 units) and 51% of under-construction unsold (4,161 units). The structural issue is product mismatch: the oversupply is almost entirely in high-rise condominiums and serviced apartments, while the landed residential market (terraced and semi-detached houses) shows healthy demand and low Overhang pressure. The market is simultaneously in expansion in Zone 1 landed and in correction in peripheral high-rise — two cycle positions sharing the same city boundary.

新山在東南亞房地產格局中佔據獨一無二的結構性地位：它是全球唯一一座與全球最貴居住市場之一以步行或短途陸路相連的廉價替代城市。新加坡私宅均價超過每平方呎SGD2,000；新山高層住宅均價約每平方呎RM450至500——換算約SGD150至165——差距逾12倍。新加坡員工月薪中位數折算逾RM20,000；新山約RM3,000至4,000。這種工資與房價套利空間，疊加即將落成的柔新捷運（RTS Link）——將跨境通勤時間從60至90分鐘壓縮至5至6分鐘——是使新山有別於其他所有馬來西亞市場的單一結構性力量，也是Zone 1 RTS走廊溢價的根本支撐。

需求論點獲三個額外的結構性支柱支撐。2025年1月正式啟動、覆蓋3,288平方公里的柔新特別經濟區（JS-SEZ），是馬來西亞一代人以來最重大的產業政策——已確認投資額已超越政府初始目標。為金融從業者提供15年零所得稅優惠的森城特別金融區（SFZ），創造了獨立於RTS通勤邏輯的高價值專業租客需求錨點。而柔佛的製造業走廊——已進駐英特爾、Western Digital及持續擴張的半導體供應鏈——提供了完全不依賴新加坡套利的穩定就業驅動需求。

OVERHANG ANATOMY: THE PRODUCT MISMATCH PROBLEM OVERHANG 解剖：產品錯配問題

Johor's Overhang problem is fundamentally a product mismatch, not a demand failure. The 21% Overhang rate is entirely a high-rise phenomenon: Condo and apartment units account for 1,029 units of the 3,293-unit residential Overhang, and the RM500K–600K band alone carries 600 units at a 21% Overhang rate. The RM1M-plus segment carries 652 units at a 23.2% Overhang rate, reflecting the specific failure of Forest City and Medini luxury high-rise projects to find buyers at developer pricing. By contrast, the landed residential market shows Overhang rates well below 10%. The most dangerous band is RM300K–400K, where the Overhang rate reaches 29.4% with an under-construction absorption rate of only 39.9% — meaning the majority of in-progress units in this band will very likely join the Overhang upon completion.

The serviced apartment dimension adds a further layer. NAPIC's commercial category records an additional 1,074 units of completed unsold serviced apartments and 1,316 under construction in Johor. The combined wide-definition Overhang — residential plus commercial SA — stands at approximately 4,367 completed unsold units, against an under-construction pipeline of 9,499 units. This total supply pressure is the central reason why selective entry discipline is essential in JB: the average statistics mask a bifurcation where one product type is deeply oversupplied and another is genuinely scarce.

柔佛的Overhang問題從根本上是產品錯配，而非需求失敗。21%的Overhang率完全是高層住宅現象：Condo和公寓佔3,293戶住宅Overhang中的1,029戶，單是RM50至60萬段就持有600戶，Overhang率21%。RM100萬以上段持有652戶，Overhang率達23.2%，反映Forest City和Medini豪宅高層項目以開發商定價尋找買家的失敗。相比之下，有地住宅市場Overhang率遠低於10%。最危險的價格段是RM30至40萬，Overhang率達29.4%，而在建吸納率僅39.9%——意味著此段大多數在建單位在完工後很可能直接加入Overhang行列。

服務式公寓的維度增加了另一層複雜性。NAPIC商業類別另計柔佛1,074戶已完工未售服務式公寓，及1,316戶在建未售。柔佛廣義Overhang——住宅加商業服務公寓——合計已完工未售約4,367戶，在建供給達9,499戶。這種總體供給壓力，正是在新山必須有選擇性進場紀律的核心原因：平均統計數據掩蓋了一種分化——一種產品類型嚴重供過於求，另一種真正稀缺。

THE RTS & JS-SEZ THESIS: WHAT'S PRICED IN, WHAT'S NOT RTS與JS-SEZ論點：哪些已定價，哪些尚未

The RTS Link is simultaneously Johor Bahru's most powerful demand catalyst and its most significant source of speculative risk. Bukit Chagar — the JB terminus station — has already seen surrounding properties appreciate from RM600–900 per square foot to RM1,100–1,300 per square foot, implying land value has more than doubled on RTS anticipation alone. At RM1,300 per square foot, JB's premier addresses are approaching the lower end of Singapore's suburban mass-market. The base case assumption is RTS completion in 2026–2027; the risk case is a further delay beyond 18 months, which would trigger a meaningful correction in the Bukit Chagar premium. Investors buying above RM1,100 per square foot in the RTS zone are effectively long the construction timeline.

The JS-SEZ thesis is more durable but slower to monetise. The SEZ encompasses 3,288 km² across seven economic corridors. The employment-to-housing demand chain typically has an 18–36 month lag. For investors with a 3–5 year horizon, the JS-SEZ employment anchor provides a structural floor for the Skudai–Tampoi–Senai–Kulai corridor that does not depend on Singapore buyers at all. This is the underappreciated part of the JB thesis: even in a scenario where the Singapore arbitrage partially deflates, the domestic employment-driven market would continue to absorb Zone 2 landed product at yields of 5.5–6.5%.

RTS Link同時是新山最強大的需求催化劑，以及最顯著的投機風險來源。武吉查卡——JB端點站——周邊物業已從每平方呎RM600至900升至RM1,100至1,300，意味著土地價值僅因RTS預期已逾倍增長。在每平方呎RM1,300水平，新山頂級地址已接近新加坡郊區大眾市場的低端。基本情境假設RTS在2026至2027年完工；風險情境是進一步延誤超過18個月，這將觸發武吉查卡溢價的顯著修正。在RTS區域以每平方呎逾RM1,100買入的投資人，實際上是在做多施工時間表。

JS-SEZ論點更具持久性但貨幣化更慢。特區涵蓋7個經濟走廊共3,288平方公里。就業到住房需求的傳導鏈通常有18至36個月的滯後期。對持有3至5年視野的投資人，JS-SEZ就業錨點為Skudai至Tampoi至Senai至Kulai走廊提供了完全不依賴新加坡買家的結構性底部。這是新山論點中被低估的部分：即便在新加坡套利部分縮窄的情境下，國內就業驅動市場仍將以5.5至6.5%的收益率持續吸收Zone 2有地產品。

MALAYSIA OVERHANG: NATIONAL PICTURE全馬OVERHANG：全國圖景

Viewed at the national level, Malaysia's property Overhang problem has three distinct structural layers that require separate analysis. The first layer is the headline residential Overhang of 28,672 units, which is meaningfully concentrated in five states: Perak (3,300), Johor (3,293), Sabah (2,771), Selangor (2,757), and Penang (2,730). Perak's Overhang is predominantly low-to-mid cost housing in a market with limited new demand catalysts — it is structural rather than cyclical and will clear slowly. Sabah's Overhang reflects the persistent mismatch between developer ambitions and local purchasing power in a frontier market. Selangor presents the paradox of simultaneously having the largest residential under-construction pipeline of any state (13,448 units) and a healthy transaction market anchored by Klang Valley tech employment — a state where the Overhang risk is real but the demand floor is structural.

The second layer — and the one most systematically ignored — is the SA Overhang of 17,892 units, where the concentration pattern is even more extreme: Johor accounts for 50.4% (9,018 units), KL 26.2% (4,687), and Selangor 11.7% (2,095). The third layer is the forward-looking supply pipeline. Selangor's SA under-construction inventory of 18,116 units will complete into a market where the existing SA Overhang is already 2,095 units. KL's planned SA inventory of 9,415 NC units represents 72.9% of the national NC total — a concentration of future supply commitment in a single market that, if approved for construction, will create a structural SA Overhang problem in KL that dwarfs the current situation. The single most important policy decision that Malaysian real estate investors should monitor is whether these 9,415 KL SA planned units receive construction approval or are withdrawn.

從全國視角看，馬來西亞的物業Overhang問題有三個截然不同的結構層次。第一層是28,672戶的表面住宅Overhang，顯著集中在五個州：霹靂（3,300）、柔佛（3,293）、沙巴（2,771）、雪蘭莪（2,757）和檳城（2,730）。霹靂的Overhang主要是缺乏新需求催化劑市場中的中低成本住宅——是結構性而非週期性的。沙巴的Overhang反映開發商野心與本地購買力之間的持久錯配。雪蘭莪呈現矛盾：同時擁有各州最大住宅在建供給（13,448戶）和以Klang Valley科技就業為錨點的健康交易市場——一個Overhang風險真實但需求底部具有結構性的州份。

第二層——也是最系統性被忽視的——是17,892戶的SA Overhang，集中程度更為極端：柔佛佔50.4%（9,018戶），KL佔26.2%（4,687戶），雪蘭莪佔11.7%（2,095戶）。第三層是前瞻性在建供給。雪蘭莪18,116戶的SA在建庫存將完工進入一個現有SA Overhang已達2,095戶的市場。KL規劃中的9,415戶SA庫存代表全國NC總量的72.9%——若獲准建設，將在KL創造使當前狀況相形見絀的結構性SA Overhang問題。馬來西亞房地產投資人應監測的最重要單一政策決定，是這9,415戶KL SA規劃單位是否獲得建設批准或被撤回。

Three infrastructure projects have moved from speculative to confirmed timelines over the past 12 months, with demand effects already measurable in transaction and rental data.

The JB–Singapore RTS, targeting operations by end-2026, has produced the most immediate impact. Rental yields in the JB Sentral corridor have risen to 5.47–5.60%, the highest in peninsular Malaysia.

The MRT3 Circle Line received final government approval in 2025, with substantial completion targeted for 2030–2031. Currently underserved corridors along the alignment (Sentul, Wangsa Maju, Seputeh, Bukit Jalil) represent the earliest-stage catalyst opportunity.

The data center investment wave — Microsoft's USD2.2 billion, Google's USD2 billion — is producing measurable spillover rental demand. Industrial property value surged 21.3% in 2025.

過去一年，有三個基礎建設項目從「說說而已」變成了「有具體時間表」，而且效果已經開始在數據裡看到了。

新山至新加坡輕軌系統（Johor Bahru–Singapore Rapid Transit System，RTS Link），目標2026年底通車。新山中央走廊的租金報酬率已升至5.47到5.60%，是馬來西亞半島最高水準，背後是新加坡人跨境買房的需求，以及柔佛-新加坡特別經濟區（Johor-Singapore Special Economic Zone，JS-SEZ）帶動的真實就業和居住需求。曾經一度看起來快變成空城的森林城市（Forest City），也因為SEZ開始出現了早期去化的跡象。

捷運三號環線（MRT3 Circle Line）在2025年拿到政府最終核准，目標2030到2031年完工。從MRT1和MRT2的經驗來看，站點周邊500公尺的物件，通常在時間表確定後12到18個月內就會開始把交通增值（Transit Premium）反映在房價上。環線沿線目前比較冷門的吉隆坡走廊——善南（Sentul）、旺沙瑪朱（Wangsa Maju）、士拍（Seputeh）、武吉加里爾（Bukit Jalil）——可能是現在最早期的布局機會。

資料中心投資潮（Data Center Wave）——微軟（Microsoft）22億美元、谷歌（Google）20億美元，加上多家雲端大廠在柔佛和雪蘭莪的大規模進駐——正在帶動周邊地區的租屋需求。2025年工業地產（Industrial Property）的成交總值年增21.3%。這個需求跟住宅景氣週期無關，持續性更有保障。

OUTLOOK AND KEY RISKS展望與主要風險

The national Overhang trajectory through 2029 is determined by three structural variables operating on different timelines. The RTS Link (2026–2027 expected commissioning) is the fastest-acting catalyst: each month of delay removes approximately 1,000–2,000 units of latent Johor absorption capacity, while commissioning would trigger the release of pent-up Singapore buyer demand. The JS-SEZ employment ramp (18–36 month lag) will define whether the 2027–2028 supply peak in Johor is met by genuine demand or accumulates further into Overhang. And the KL SA planned pipeline decision (9,415 NC units) is the longest-dated but potentially largest single risk: if approved, it would extend KL's SA Overhang problem from the current 4,687 units toward 15,000+ units by 2030.

The base case (50% probability) projects national residential Overhang stabilising near 29,500 units in 2026 before declining to approximately 18,000 units by 2029, as the UC pipeline completes and new starts remain disciplined. SA Overhang follows a similar but slower trajectory, remaining above 17,000 units through 2027 before declining toward 11,000 by 2029. The bull case (25% probability) requires RTS to commission on schedule and JS-SEZ to attract RM50 billion-plus, driving Overhang toward 10,000 residential units and 6,500 SA units by 2029. The bear case (20% probability) — RTS delayed, KL SA pipeline approved, global risk-off — would see national residential Overhang reach 38,000–40,000 units by 2028 and SA Overhang approaching 25,000 units. The key variable to monitor is not price — prices in Malaysia are sticky downward — but transaction volume: a sustained decline below 90,000 units quarterly would be the clearest leading indicator that the bear case trajectory is being entered.
